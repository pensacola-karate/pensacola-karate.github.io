Kata

Taikyoku Shodan

Taikyoku Shodan (First Cause, First Level) is the most basic of Shotokan forms, it has 20 moves, and it is primarily used to teach beginners the fundamental aspects of kata. The name of the Kata is a reference ot the Karate maxim: "In Karate, there is no advantage in the first attack". In this Kata there are only down blocks (gedan-barai), body lunge punches (chudan oi-tzuki), and forward stances (zenkutsu dachi). "Shouts" (Kiai) are traditionally performed at movements 8# and 16#.

Heian Shodan

Heian Shodan (Peaceful Mind, level one) is the first form teached of the Heian series. This form has 21 movements and it is very similar to the Taikyoku series with the addition of vertical hammer-fist strike (kentsui-tate-mawashi-uchi), rising block (age-uke), knife-hand block (shuto-uke), and back-stance (kokutsu-dachi). Kiai are traditionally performed at movement 9# and 17#.

Heian Nidan

Heian Nidan (Peaceful Mind, level two) is the second form teached of the Heian series. This form has 26 movements and it introduces several movement patterns such as the back of forearm block/forearm forehead posture (haiwan-uke/zenwan-hitae-kamae), outside flowing block /outside hammer-fist strike (soto-nagashi uke /zentsui-soto-mawashi-uchi), side punch (sokumen-zuki), fist to hip posture with side kick and punch (ryoken-koshi-kamae and uraken-yoko-mawashi-uuchi/yoko-geri-keage), spear hand with pressing block (shihon-tate-nukite / osae-uke), semi front stance (ashi/hanmi zenkutsu dachi), and front kick (mae geri). Kiai are traditionally performed at movement 11# and 26#.

Heian Sandan

Heian Sandan (Peaceful Mind, level three) is the third form teached of the Heian series. This form has 20 movements and it introduces several movement patterns such as the horse stance (kiba dachi), body shifting (tai sabaki), elbow strikes (empi). This is the first kata that contains slow moments. Kiai are traditionally performed at movement 10# and 20#.

Heian Yondan

Heian Yondan (Peaceful Mind, level four) is the fourth form teached of the Heian series. This form has 27 movements and it primarily based on kokutsu-dachi and the cross stance (kosa-dachi). Kiai are traditionally performed at movement 13# and 25#.

Heian Godan

Heian Yondan (Peaceful Mind, level fifth) is the last form teached of the Heian series. This form has 23 movements and it is a combination of quick and slow movements. It is also the first kata introducing a jump (tobi-komi). Kiai are traditionally performed at movement 12# and 19#.
